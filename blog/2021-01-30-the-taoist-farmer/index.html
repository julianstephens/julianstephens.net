The Taoist Farmer

There’s a Taoist parable of a farmer whose horse runs away. When his neighbors find out, they offer condolences for his bad luck. The farmer simply replies “We’ll see”. Soon the horse returns with four more horses, and when this time he is praised for his good luck, the farmer replies “We’ll see.” The farmer’s son sets to work breaking in the horses but falls and breaks his leg. Once again, the neighbors lament the farmer’s bad luck only to receive the same reply, “We’ll see.” When soldiers arrive at the village a few days later to draft all able-bodied men, the farmer’s son is spared due to his broken leg.

I’ve had this parable in the corner of my mind constantly for the last few years. College at the best of times has felt like a roller coaster with faulty breaks. A shift in any direction could spell disaster or good fortune. As I get caught up in the anxiety of classes, internships, grad school, and finding a job during a global pandemic, I try to continually remind myself that few things are ever as bad or good as they initially seem.

Deep breath in to the count of four, long breath out to the count of seven. Remember the farmer going about his work prepared to weather the storms and ride out the highs, all with a peaceful acceptance of what is. The world around you may panic, but you have a choice of whether to participate. Choose balance. Keep your head level, do good work. Till your fields so that you’re welcoming when good fortune visits and well-prepared when misfortune strikes.
