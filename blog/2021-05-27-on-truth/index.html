On Truth

There are a lot of things that I used to know for a fact. The older I get, the more inclined I am to adopt a position of scientific ambivalence. I’ve learned and unlearned so many things over the last decade that I can only assume the pursuit of absolutes is a fickle one.

There does, however, appear to be at least one objective truth that holds across time. Human nature itself is the ultimate source of bias. To be a living, conscious being is to continually engage in the act of constructing realities, of constructing truths. The problem is not that we’re all living in vastly different worlds; for the most part, they tend to align neatly. The real issue lays in the fact that as we age, we creep towards certainties even as we realize their shortcomings.

Having understood this, I find myself compelled to fight, as hard as I can, that slouching march towards certitude. So far the most effective technique I’ve found is to unapologetically change my mind. The hordes on social platforms seem to find this simple act a cardinal sin. However, in my experience so far, it’s the most valuable tactic I have. Maybe I’m just young and na√Øve, but the idea of truly knowing anything seems absurd. For now, I strive simply to gather as much knowledge as possible and hope that someday I might find within its depths a truth that I feel content to cling to.
